# US-009: Search candidates with multiple criteria, certifications and impressiveness scoring

> **Epic:** [EPIC-04](../epics/EPIC-04.md) — Candidate Search & CV Search  
> **Priority:** P1 (Must)  
> **Story Points:** 8  
> **Sprint:** Sprint 3  
> **Status:** New  
> **Business Requirement:** BR-01

## Description

As a **Manager/Account Lead**, I want to search for internal candidates using multiple criteria — including one or several skills and/or certifications combined in a single query, together with other attributes such as languages, provider location, job level, service line, and MECES/EQF level — and I want results to include an impressiveness score per candidate based on certifications, industry experience, and seniority, in order to quickly identify the most suitable and most compelling profiles for a tender, agreement, or RFI response.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] When filters from at least two categories are applied, the result set updates to show matching candidates
- [ ] Multiple skills and/or certifications can be selected simultaneously and the search returns only candidates who meet the combined requirements (based on the configured matching logic)
- [ ] Results also include candidates with potential to obtain the missing certifications and complete the requested profile
- [ ] Adding or removing a skill/certification filter updates results without losing other applied filters
- [ ] When opening a candidate's summary, the key attributes used in the selection are visible (location, job level, certifications, languages, EQF/MECES level)
- [ ] Each candidate is assigned an impressiveness score calculated from certifications (count and relevance), industry experience (years), and seniority/job level
- [ ] The impressiveness score is shown as a sortable column and results can be ranked by it
- [ ] Hovering or expanding a candidate row reveals the score breakdown by factor
- [ ] Scoring weights per factor are configurable by an administrator; weights must sum to 100% and changes take effect on the next search
